The method

Process, enforced by code

Retail strategies mostly die of self-deception — lookahead, cost amnesia, moving goalposts. The defense is procedural, not aspirational:

01

Pre-registration

Hypothesis, exact spec (one variant — no parameter fishing), cost model, and pass/fail gate are frozen into the log before any result exists. The verdict branches are written down in advance.

02

Honest engine invariants

The backtest engine refuses zero costs, executes at t+1 (lookahead structurally impossible, regression-tested), and auto-splits out-of-sample.

03

Negative results are first-class

Closures are published with explicit reopen conditions, so dead ideas stay dead until the world actually changes. Two data-corruption incidents are documented as full postmortems.

04

Forward records over backtests

Passing a gate earns a paper book only. Promotion requires the forward record itself to clear statistical adjudication — a backtest is never the final word.

Forward records are short and noisy, so the inference layer is explicit and frozen ahead of time:

bootstrap CI

Confidence intervals on live Sharpe — a 6-day record is never quoted as if it were evidence.

permutation test

"Is this drawdown luck?" — answered by resampling, not by feel.

A/B adjudicator

Strategy variants run a full quarter in parallel; promotion is decided by a pre-committed rule.

decay state machine

Frozen alpha-decay thresholds; two consecutive warning weeks force a formal review.

Case study · E65

Passing the gate is necessary, not sufficient

The most recent experiment shows the discipline working end-to-end.

1

Hypothesis, frozen first. Deep-discount convertible bonds embed a "downward-revision" option — issuers cut the conversion price to dodge the put. Spec, costs, gate, and an attribution plan were pre-registered before the backtest ran.

2

The gate passed cleanly. Post-2021: net +10.7%/yr, Sharpe 1.31, maxDD −8.3% — robust to doubled slippage and to excluding the best year. A weaker process would have shipped it here.

3

Then the pre-committed attribution ran. Only 16% of returns came from actual revision events; 84% was generic deep-discount beta. And the monthly returns correlated 0.77 with an existing book — despite ~0% holdings overlap.

Verdict: gate recorded as passed — no retroactive goalpost-moving — but the direction was closed as redundant rather than funded, with reopen conditions on record.

Engineering

Hardened against hostile data

Free data sources fail in creative ways. Incidents become permanent, mechanical defenses — never just a manual fix:

  • Write-time circuit breaker After an adjusted-price convention violation created fake −99% cliffs: any refresh implying >50% single-day moves across >10% of names is rejected before it touches disk. Contaminated rows were quarantined, never deleted.
  • No fill at a non-finite price After NaN OHLC rows cascaded into a book: adapters drop close-less bars, and the paper trader freezes positions on any non-finite price — a fail-safe shared by all nine books, locked in by a sentinel test.
  • Hard wall-clock timeouts on every external call After a silent API hang froze a book for hours — no network call runs unbounded.
  • Five-layer execution safety Account pinning → per-order caps → exchange reconciliation flags → weight/gross clamps → a drawdown circuit breaker requiring human review to clear. Real-money switches belong to the human; none have been flipped.
